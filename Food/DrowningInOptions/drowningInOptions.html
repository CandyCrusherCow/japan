Unforgettable Food in Tokyo

Written by Babbling Gaijin

Ramen Tatsunoya Shinjuku

We visited this ramen shop in Japan, and it was the first and last place we ate at during our trip. Despite having to wait for an hour, which seemed to be the norm no matter what time we went, we can confidently say that it was one of the best ramen shops we visited in Japan.

The majority of the restaurants we visited in Tokyo were only good enough for one visit. However, this particular restaurant was so delicious that we ate there three times during our six-day stay.

Don't forget about dessert. The pudding is so creamy I didn't even think a ramen shop could nail ramen and dessert but you're missing out if you don't get dessert.

Ramen is one of the least expensive meals you can get in Japan, for this price it's a mind-blowing experience.

Shinjuku Haruta-ya

We stumbled upon this place while exploring Shinjuku. It seemed popular with locals so we thought we would give it a try. It's a standing yakitori place with cheap food, beer, and many options. I loved the pork belly skewers and summer corn dish. If you are feeling adventurous but don't want to spend a lot this is a perfect place to explore.

Rokurinsha

Another ramen shop, this one is located in the train station! The ramen here was completely different from the first shop. This shop specializes in dip noodles, which have thicker noodles and a curry base broth. Despite the hefty wait time of an hour and a half mid-day, the portions are massive and delicious!

Pro Tip: Finding this place was hard, we tried to navigate the underground train station with little to no directions. But, If you follow the signs to the JR exit you will soon find a mini-mall connected to the mall a place called Ramen Avenue where they have clustered several Ramen shops including this one.

Tsukiji Fish Market

The food here is incredible if you know where to go! We had everything from Wagu beef, grilled eel, fresh-cut tuna, torched tuna, raw scallops and so much more.

This place is massive and overwhelming. Thus, this is one of the few places that I heavily recommend getting a tour guide. I honestly was hesitant about getting a guide however upon arriving at the market was very thankful we had one! "Tsukiji Private Tour: Eat Every Street Food & See Everything", I loved this tour because no food was included so the guide would take you to the best places and you could decide what you wanted instead of having a pre-planned menu. I am not a picky eater, however, I have a small appetite. So this kind of tour is perfect for me. In addition, this is a private tour only so the guide tailored our tour to our interests and preferences. After experiencing the tour we went again without a guide and were extremely lost and tried food that wasn't the greatest so the tour was worth it!

P.S. While there I tried documenting all the amazing places we ate at the fish market but none of it is documented online and all the names are in Japanese.

馳走 風々（ちそう ふふ

Kaiseki is a whole segment of its own. This restaurant was an incredible fine dining experience. We had everything from grilled salmon to sashimi of fish I didn't even know existed. The food was incredible but what made the experience special was the people. Although none of the staff spoke any English they were very accommodating in trying to explain everything using pictures and Google Translate. In the intimate restaurant, we were the only people in there. This was an astounding experience that I will always treasure.

Sadly we took no pictures but if you would like to see some there are plenty on Google Maps and if you want to learn more about kaiseki click here

Little Bakery Shinjuku

We mostly went to convenience stores for breakfast but the last few days we found this bakery that we just adored. They had a large assortment of buns, doughnuts and sandwiches. My personal favourite was a flaky doughnut that was filled with Taro paste.
