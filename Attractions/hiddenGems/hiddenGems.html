Hidden Gems of Japan

Written by Babbling Gaijin

Japan has so many different landscapes and cultures, there is so much more to explore!.

Based on personal experience these were my favourite places to visit that were less crowded.

Tokyo: Jimbōchō station

I only found out about this place because I read a book about it, despite all my research it never came up. Jimbocho is the largest collection of second hand book stores in the world. I would recommend going there after 10am. Each store is dedicated to a different genre. If you're lucky you may chat to a bookstore owner about their bookstore! Jimbocho offers lots of stationary stores, coffee shops and pastry shops. It's my literal heaven.

Tokyo: Roka Koshun-en Gardens

This is the first garden we visited in Japan. The main reason we went there was for the bamboo garden. This little gem in the city is where I got some fantastic and beautiful shots in the bamboo garden. Although it is very small, upon arrival nobody was there and we got to enjoy it all by ourselves. A little bonus was a historical house that is maintained. Best part was it was free and empty.

Tokyo: Shimokitazawa(thrift store district)

This may be one of the busier Gems in the lot depending on where and when you start, but it was definitely my favorite. If you love thrifting this is definitely the place to go!

Tokyo: Ushiku Daibutsu

This is 2hrs away(by transit) from the city centre. It's the largest Buddha statue in Japan and was absolutely gorgeous. We got there first thing in the morning and nobody was there. You can enter the statue (no shoes allowed) to see some wonderful exhibits inside.

Takayama

This little onsen town completely stole my heart. In the middle of nowhere we landed at our hotel in the middle of the mountains. Fantastic for a romantic getaway from the city life/ crowds. The views are breathtaking. If you want to know more click here.

Kyoto:Rengeoin Sanjusangendo

This national treasure is still a hidden treasure in Japan. Way less crowded compared to its counterparts Inari shrine, and nijo castle. We almost skipped this one because our schedule was so packed, but so glad we didn't because it was the highlight of our day. No cameras allowed and the golden statues are insanely beautiful.

Yakushima

Many of the islands off Japan seem extraordinary. However, I was entranced by yakushima. It looked like a magical place forged in a fantasy novel. It was incredible and extremely far which is why so few go there. If you want to know more click here.
